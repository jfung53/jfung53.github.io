Are residential eviction rates higher in areas with more single mothers?

ArcGIS R US Census NYC Open Data

The answer: without a doubt.

Heat map of residential evictions and rates of single mothers in NYC, 2024.

The NYC Open Data Portal publishes a detailed dataset on pending, scheduled, and executed evictions within the five boroughs. I wanted to see how eviction patterns affect women, especially single mothers.

 

According to the 2012 study Eviction and the Reproduction of Urban Poverty, eviction is to women what incarceration is to men, a typical, systemic occurrence that affects poor black women disproportionately.

Data

I removed all commercial evictions and was left with 15,473 residential evictions in 2024. I mapped this data as a heat map in ArcGIS and found hot spots in areas of the Bronx, and Flatbush, East New York, and Brownsville in Brooklyn.

 

I brought in US Census data (ACS 2022) to get the percentage of single mothers per census tract, encoding this as circle size.

Results

The map suggests that there is indeed a relationship between residential evictions and higher rates of single motherhood. This supports this explanation of eviction patterns that says eviction is a spatially clustered, endemic feature of neighborhood disadvantage, especially in a city like New York.
